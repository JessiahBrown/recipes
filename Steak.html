Pan Seared Steak

Pan-seared steak is a method of cooking steak where a thick cut of beef is quickly cooked in a hot skillet or frying pan. The steak is seasoned with salt and pepper (and optionally other spices or herbs), then seared on both sides to develop a flavorful brown crust while keeping the interior tender and juicy. This technique allows for precise control over the steak's doneness, from rare to well-done, depending on personal preference. Pan-seared steak is often finished with butter and aromatic herbs like thyme or rosemary for added flavor and richness. It's a popular cooking method due to its simplicity, speed, and the delicious results it produces.

Ingredients

  • Your preferred cut of steak
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Onion Powder
  • Garlic Powder
  • Rosemary
  • Thyme
  • Butter

Steps

  1. Pat steak dry and allow to get to room temperature.
  2. Season both sides of the steak with salt, pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder.
  3. Preheat the pan on medium and add oil
  4. Add steaks and sear each side fpr 3-4 minutes or until a brown crust has formed, then use tongs to turn steaks on their sides and sear the edges for 1 minute each
  5. Reduce heat
  6. Add butter, rosemary, and thyme to the pan, then baste the steak in the Butter
  7. Once steak has reached your desires temperature, remove and allow to rest for 10 minutes
  8. After 10 minutes cut into the steak and Enjoy!
